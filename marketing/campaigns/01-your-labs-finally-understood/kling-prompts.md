# Campaign 1 — Kling 3.0 Video Prompts (copy-paste into Higgsfield)

> Ready-to-paste text-to-video prompts for **Higgsfield → Kling 3.0 · 5s · 720p
> · 9:16 (vertical)**. One prompt per scene from
> [`scripts.md`](./scripts.md). These render the **motion/background plates
> only** — all on-screen text (captions, CTA, URL, and the Scene 6 disclaimer)
> is burned in afterward in post (CapCut/Premiere), because t2v models
> hallucinate text/numbers and a hallucinated dose or "diagnosis" word would
> violate marketing [`GOLD.md`](../../GOLD.md) §2.
>
> **Settings to confirm before each Generate:** Model = Kling 3.0 (GENERAL
> preset) · Duration = 5s · Resolution = 720p · Ratio = 9:16.
>
> **Compliance:** every prompt is written to avoid medication imagery, doses,
> vials, faces, bodies, and before/after transformations. Use **synthetic**
> placeholder lab values only — never a real patient's numbers.

## Scene 1 — The folder

```
Vertical 9:16, top-down shot of a cluttered desk, slow cinematic push-in. Scattered printed laboratory result PDFs from different clinics with different letterheads, slightly desaturated, blurred synthetic numbers visible, no personal names. A smartphone lies beside the pile, screen off. Overcast soft window light, shallow depth of field, dust motes. Tired, messy, realistic. No faces, no people, no medications, no vials. Slow steady zoom.
```

## Scene 2 — The shift

```
Vertical 9:16, same desk now clean and bright. Eye-level on a smartphone resting on the desk. Loose paper documents sweep and dissolve off-frame. The phone screen turns on revealing a clean, minimal vertical timeline UI sliding in from the left; small file-format chips labeled PDF, JPG, PNG, HEIC pop in; a soft glowing plus-upload button is tapped by a thumb. Bright modern UI, soft shadows, calm confident mood. No medical text, no diagnoses.
```

## Scene 3 — Normalized

```
Vertical 9:16, close-up screen-recording style of a clean dark mobile UI, slight motion-tracked handheld tilt. Two lab result cards from different clinics slide in side by side, each showing placeholder numeric values and units, clearly synthetic. They smoothly animate and align onto one shared normalized axis; a small green normalized pill switches on for each. Minimal, precise, data-visualization aesthetic. No patient names, no flagged results, no doses.
```

## Scene 4 — Trends

```
Vertical 9:16, hero line graph filling a phone screen, slow push-in. Horizontal axis is dates, vertical axis is values. A clean trend line draws from left to right. A soft shaded reference-range band fades in behind it. Two overlay toggles click on: a thin medication track showing timing ticks only, and a symptoms track with small dots. Modern clinical dashboard, muted green in-range and amber accents, never alarm-red. Smooth, confident, the visual peak of the video. No numeric doses anywhere.
```

## Scene 5 — The report

```
Vertical 9:16, a hand lifts a smartphone showing a scrolling clean structured report with clear section headers, then sets it down beside a notebook and a coffee cup on a wooden desk. Warm natural side light, shallow depth of field, cinematic. On the phone, a footer line reads like a code hash next to a Reproducible badge. Calm, resolving, professional mood. No medication names, no dosages, no diagnoses anywhere on screen.
```

## Scene 6 — Close

In the background, the timeline-and-graph motif from the previous scene drifts slowly and fades into the brand color. Clean, confident, premium finish, subtle film grain. Hold still, loop-friendly.
