## Strategy

**Audience:** Physicians, endocrinologists, urologists, men's-health and TRT
clinicians, and clinic staff who review patient-supplied lab histories.

**Core promise (compliant):** Your patient arrives with a **structured,
traceable briefing** — normalized timeline, per-lab ranges, trend + red-flag
sweep — so the visit starts at the decision, not the re-read.

**What we do NOT promise:** that the tool prescribes, diagnoses, or replaces
clinical judgment (GOLD §2.1). The clinician is always the decision-maker.

## Channels & format

- **Primary:** LinkedIn (long-form post + 6-slide carousel), Doximity.
- **Secondary:** Clinician newsletter, conference handout (PDF).
- **Format:** 6 scenes adapted to carousel slides + a ~45s walkthrough video.
  Burned-in captions. AA contrast. Light + dark safe.

## KPIs

- LinkedIn post impressions ≥ 20k; carousel swipe-through ≥ 30%.
- Demo requests from clinicians ≥ 1% of clicks.
- Sample-report downloads ≥ 50/campaign.
- Zero §2 violations in clinical-safety review.

## Compliance notes

- Closing scene (Scene 6) carries the on-screen disclaimer; **full disclaimer**
  in the post body.
- Never imply the product issues a diagnosis or a dosing recommendation.
- "Red flags for review" = prompts for clinician attention, **not** diagnoses.
- Emphasize **deterministic**: same data → same report (sha256). This is the
  differentiator vs. "AI told me" tools.
- Check LinkedIn health/medical ad policy before sponsoring.
